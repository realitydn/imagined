About The Imagined

What do we see when we cannot see? And how do images emerge from words?

The Imagined is an exhibition without paintings. In their place — 21 works based on children's descriptions of well-known artworks.

These descriptions are not filtered through the lens of art history or visual education. The children interpret freely — with imagination, honesty, and their own language. Some are literal, some poetic, some wildly inventive.

No images, only language. Each piece invites us to "see" a painting through someone else's eyes — in this case, a child's. Recognizing the artwork becomes an act of interpretation: we reconstruct it within ourselves, guided not by visual cues, but by memory, cultural codes, and the strange accuracy of a child's perspective.

Idea and concept: Nasta Ion (Ion Playground)

Presented and supported by: REALITY Da Nang

Here, language becomes a way of seeing. And imagination — the primary tool of perception.
